Popular Destinations in India

Delhi - The Heart of India

Delhi, India's capital territory, is a massive metropolitan area in the north of the country. In Old Delhi, a 17th-century Mughal-era enclave, stands the imposing Red Fort, a symbol of India, and the sprawling Jama Masjid mosque, whose courtyard accommodates 25,000 people.

Its historic sites, museums, galleries, gardens and exotic shows, make it a true capital of culture. From historical monuments to bustling shopping malls, and from a vibrant nightlife to mouth-watering cuisines, Delhi has a lot to offer. The city is also home to three UNESCO World Heritage Sites - the Qutub Minar, Humayun's Tomb, and the Red Fort.

Rajasthan - Land of Kings

Rajasthan, India's largest state by area, is a land of vibrant culture, majestic forts, and opulent palaces. Known for its desert landscape and rich history, it's a popular tourist destination.

Cities like Jaipur (the Pink City), Udaipur (the City of Lakes), and Jodhpur (the Blue City) offer unique experiences. Explore ancient Havelis, vibrant bazaars, and experience the warm hospitality of Rajasthan.

Kerala - God's Own Country

Kerala, a state on India's tropical Malabar Coast, has nearly 600km of Arabian Sea shoreline. It's known for its palm-lined beaches and backwaters, a network of canals.

Inland are the Western Ghats, mountains whose slopes support tea, coffee and spice plantations as well as wildlife. National parks like Eravikulam and Periyar, plus Wayanad and other sanctuaries, are home to elephants, langur monkeys and tigers.
